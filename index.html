Hello, my name is Bhavindu.
I’m an undergraduate student from OCAD University completing a BDES in Environmental Design with a specialization in architecture. Before coming to architecture, I studied fine arts. This background in fine arts continues to inspire and inform my architectural designs insofar as all my designs are founded through a rigorous process of drawn ideation. For me, a successful design solves problems in beautiful and innovative ways and is ultimately the culmination of innumerable moments of thought and care.

My experience in professional work thus far includes internship and freelance work. I have completed 1 internship (4 month duration) at Van Ziffle Architects, along with 3 freelance projects, namely, 1 project at Addeo Studio Architecture and 2 client-driven architectural projects. Beyond such work, I partook in an international architecture competition: The Big Thing – Architecture of the Abandoned, where I achieved an honourable mention, ranking 14th worldwide from over 4000 submissions.

Aside from architecture and design, I’ve represented Canada as a professional cricket player at both junior and senior levels. I played for the Canadian national men’s cricket team at the age of 16 and captained the Canadian under 19 team during numerous international competitions.

In my pastime, I enjoy playing board games (Monopoly, Risk, Ticket to Ride, and Catan) and watching football (Lionel Messi is my favourite player). Recently, I’ve also started to play chess, mostly because my brothers love it.
