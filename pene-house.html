THE PENÉ HOUSE
LOCATION: SCARBOROUGH, TORONTO, ON.
PROJECT TYPE: INDIVIDUAL ACADEMIC WORK
DURATION: 3 MONTHS (FALL 2020 SEMESTER)
The Pené House was designed as a personal habitat for an occupant, facilitating their requirements for off-grid living. The project’s challenge was to discover solutions for all aspects of basic off-grid living including all systematic requirements such as heating/cooling, water management, etc. The difficulty with this project was not finding technical solutions for how each system would function. Rather, it was defining the narrative for the architectural design and figuring out how to embed my architectural design within the chosen site. To define the narrative, I chose to explore the notion of biomimicry: the imitation of natural biological design or processes. Since the project’s location was overlooking Lake Ontario, I chose to imitate the fractal formulations present in water.
