THE TIBETAN REVIVAL
The essence of the Tibetan Revival project was to develop a design solution for a community center at the prescribed location: Parkdale’s King and Dufferin intersection. The challenge of this project was to discover meaning in the design proposal, a reason for its manifestation. Insofar as the location of this project was defined, I sought to figure out the reason for its selection. Following extensive research on the site’s history, the overwhelming Tibetan presence in that area became abundantly clear. In spite of this presence, there lacked notable remnants of Tibetan identity, history, and culture as they have fallen victim to homogeneity. And so developed the reasons for my design proposal’s manifestation: an architectural solution that employs the theoretical framework of Critical Regionalism to revive the lost Tibetan identity and to challenge the existing homogeneity.
